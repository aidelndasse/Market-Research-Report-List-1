<p><ul><li>Low Permeability</li><li>High Permeability</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Microwave absorber foam is categorized into low and high permeability types based on their ability to allow air flow through the material. Low permeability foams are dense and designed to absorb microwaves effectively, minimizing signal reflection and interference, making them suitable for applications requiring high attenuation. Conversely, high permeability foams are more porous, allowing better airflow and are often used in environments where heat dissipation is crucial, such as in electronics packaging or wireless communication systems.</p></p>
