<p><p>The urinary incontinence treatment devices market includes several key types. Artificial urinary sphincters provide a mechanical solution to improve bladder control by mimicking natural muscle functionality. Electrical stimulation devices stimulate nerves to enhance bladder function and control. Urethral slings offer support to the urethra, preventing involuntary leakage during physical activities. Catheters are used for temporary or long-term drainage of urine, assisting individuals with severe incontinence in managing their condition effectively. Each device plays a vital role in enhancing patient quality of life.</p></p>
