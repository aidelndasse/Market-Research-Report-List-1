<p><p>The conductive glass substrate market primarily consists of three types: FTO (Fluorine-doped Tin Oxide), ITO (Indium Tin Oxide), and other materials. FTO is known for its high transparency and conductivity, making it suitable for solar cells and displays. ITO offers superior optical clarity and electrical performance, widely used in touchscreens and OLEDs. Other substrates may include alternatives like carbon nanotubes or silver nanowires, catering to niche applications in flexible electronics and advanced photovoltaic systems, enhancing innovation and market diversity.</p></p>
