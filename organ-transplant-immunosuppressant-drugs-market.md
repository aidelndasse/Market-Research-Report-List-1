<p><p>The organ transplant immunosuppressant drugs market consists of various types, each serving a specific function in preventing organ rejection. Antibodies target specific immune cells to reduce rejection risk. Antiproliferative agents inhibit cell division, limiting immune response. Calcineurin inhibitors block T-cell activation, critical in immune response. mTOR inhibitors interfere with cellular growth and proliferation, while steroids suppress overall immune activity. Together, these drugs enable successful organ transplants by ensuring the recipient's immune system recognizes the transplanted organ.</p></p>
