<p><p>The multi-band wireless access points market is categorized mainly into dual-band and tri-band types. Dual-band access points operate on two frequency bands, typically 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz, allowing for flexible connectivity and reduced interference. Tri-band access points, on the other hand, add an additional 5 GHz band, enhancing performance by providing more capacity for connected devices. These distinctions enable better handling of network congestion and improved overall user experience in environments with high device density.</p></p>
