<p><ul><li>Static Compression Therapy</li><li>Dynamic Compression Therapy</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Compression therapy encompasses two main types: static and dynamic compression therapy. Static compression therapy involves the application of consistent pressure through wraps, bandages, or garments to reduce swelling and improve circulation. This method is commonly used in managing conditions like lymphedema and venous insufficiency. In contrast, dynamic compression therapy employs devices that generate varying pressure, promoting enhanced blood flow and lymphatic drainage. This approach is often utilized in rehabilitation settings and for post-surgical recovery, offering greater mobility and comfort.</p></p>
